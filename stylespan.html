Context

This project was completed during my time as an Interaction Designer at IDEO. I was tasked with revisiting a previous IDEO initiative—Beyond the Bag, a service design project focused on reducing plastic waste in the shopping experience—and approaching the challenge through a new lens.

My role as Interaction Designer was to lead the exploration of the problem, conduct user research to uncover key pain points and opportunity areas, and design a new service experience that met the needs of our consumers.

Problem

Research

We mapped the journey of plastic waste across the fashion supply chain—from raw material production to packaging to disposal—and quickly realized the greatest opportunity lay in consumption. By shifting behaviour at the source, we could prevent plastic waste before it ever entered the system.

To build a solution grounded in real user needs, we conducted 12 interviews to explore the emotions, habits, and decisions behind clothing purchases. Through careful synthesis, we uncovered three key insights that shaped our design strategy.

Ideation

How might we empower shoppers to practice mindful consumption by offering them tangible insights into the value of their wardrobe choices?

Brainstorming a wide range of ideas, we narrowed down to three early concepts that explored different approaches to this challenge. Using sketches and concept cards, we tested these prototypes with users and defined a set of design principles to guide our final concept.

Concept

And so we created StyleSpan—a smart clothing tag system paired with a companion app that helps users track their wardrobe habits and understand the true value of their items over time.

By revealing hidden patterns in their closet, StyleSpan empowers users to make more mindful, intentional choices about what they buy and wear.
