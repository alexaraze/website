CAPTCHA_IRL

 Art Installation   Electronics   Internet of Things 

An interactive art installation that reimagines the CAPTCHA test as a physical, human-verification ritual. Presented at the Fabricated Reality exhibition at the University of Pennsylvania.

Participants respond to open-ended prompts by touching one of nine abstract bouba/kiki shapes—ambiguous forms open to interpretation. Each interaction triggers LED feedback and logs a response via IFTTT, revealing new prompts and updating real-time survey data. Built with Arduino, capacitive sensors, Neopixels, and IoT integrations, the system gathers these abstract selections into a pattern—creating a collective visualization of human intuition.
